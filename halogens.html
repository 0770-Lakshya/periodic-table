Halogens are nonmetals found in Group 17 of the periodic table.

>>Halogens are highly reactive elements that readily form salts with metals. They exist in all three states of matter at room temperature and include some of the most reactive nonmetals. Their reactivity decreases down the group.

  • Fluorine (F)
  • Chlorine (Cl)
  • Bromine (Br)
  • Iodine (I)
  • Astatine (At)
  • Tennessine (Ts)
